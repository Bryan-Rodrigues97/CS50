According to the debugger's rubber duck: CS50 is an introductory course to computer science offered by Harvard University. It's designed for students with or without prior programming experience. The course covers a broad range of foundational concepts in computer science, including algorithms, data structures, software engineering, and web development. It's suitable for anyone interested in gaining a deeper understanding of how computer technology works.


Only students at Harvard can take the course?

No, not at all. While CS50 is indeed offered at Harvard, the course is also available to the public for free through edX, an online learning platform.

The 2024 course schedule is divided into 11 weeks, as follows:

  • Week 0 - Scratch
  • Week 1 - C
  • Week 2 - Arrays
  • Week 3 - Algorithms
  • Week 4 - Memory
  • Week 5 - Data Structures
  • Week 6 - Python
  • Week 7 - SQL
  • Week 8 - HTML, CSS, JavaScript
  • Week 9 - Flask
  • Week 10 - Cybersecurity
